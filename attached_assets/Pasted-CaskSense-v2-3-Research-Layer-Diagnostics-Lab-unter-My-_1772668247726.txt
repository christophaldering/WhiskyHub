CaskSense v2.3 — “Research Layer / Diagnostics Lab” unter My Taste (Pro Mode, ohne Feature-Verlust)

KONTEXT
Repo: CaskSense v2.0.0 (React 18 + Vite + TS, Wouter, Zustand, React Query, i18next, Dark Warm Theme, shadcn/ui)
Backend/DB: Express + Drizzle + Postgres (28 Tabellen: participants, tastings, whiskies, ratings, journal_entries, whiskybase_collection, wishlist_entries, tasting_participants, etc.)
Bewertungen: 5 Dimensionen (Nose, Taste, Finish, Balance, Overall), Context-Level (Naked/Self/Full)
Ziel: CaskSense als “Tasting-Tool mit Intelligenz” (2 Screens) bleibt klar – aber wir wollen einen tiefen Research/Methodik-Bereich für Entdecker, inkl. Benchmarks, multivariate Analysen, Messtheorie, Diagnostik-Notizen.
WICHTIG: Keine Funktionalität entfernen. Keine DB-Schema-Änderungen. Kein Backend-API Entfernen.

ZIEL (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
1) Unter “My Taste” einen “Research / Diagnostics Lab” Bereich anlegen.
2) Dieser Bereich ist standardmäßig NICHT prominent, sondern “Advanced / Research” (tief im Menü).
3) Inhalte dürfen überraschend/beeindruckend sein (für Psychologie/Methodik-affine Nutzer).
4) Inhalte sollen mit existierender Datenstruktur arbeiten (ratings, tastings, participants etc.).
5) Pro Mode Toggle, reversibel, ohne DB-Änderung (LocalStorage).

SCOPE
- Frontend-only: neue Seiten/Komponenten, Routing, Navigation-Hooks, i18n-Strings.
- Datenabruf nur über existierende API(s) – falls Daten fehlen: graceful empty states + “needs more data” Hinweis, KEIN Backend-Bau.
- Visuals: Dark Warm + Apple-Style Komponenten (leise, premium). Kein Redesign des Gesamtsystems nötig.

B) Benchmarks & Norms page
Goal: make the “uncopiable value” visible.
Sections:
- “Personal Norms” (your mean/SD per dimension, per context-level)
- “Stability Over Time” (month-by-month mean drift)
- “Context Sensitivity” (Naked vs Full deltas)
- “Panel Positioning” (if community aggregates exist: your percentile; else show placeholder + “coming soon”)

Charts:
- simple line chart (overall over time)
- box-like summary (mean ± sd) per dimension
No seaborn; use existing chart libs (Recharts if already used).

C) Multivariate Models page (the fun “Sozialforschung”-wow)
Provide 4 modules (even if some are “preview” depending on data):
1) “Flavor Space (PCA-lite)”:
   - If enough data: compute a simple PCA client-side on standardized 5-dim ratings (Nose/Taste/Finish/Balance/Overall)
   - Show 2D scatter of drams, color-coded by whisky type if available (or by context-level)
   - If PCA library not present, implement a lightweight PCA via small JS (or fallback to “correlation heatmap”).
2) “Clustering (Taste Archetypes)”:
   - k-means lite on standardized ratings; show 3–5 clusters “The Smoke-Lover”, “The Sweet Balancer”, etc.
   - Important: present as playful labels + neutral academic disclaimer.
3) “Predictive heuristics”:
   - simple regression-like explanation: “Which dimension best predicts Overall for you?”
   - compute correlations between dimensions and Overall; show ranking.
4) “Palate Drift Detector”:
   - detect whether your mean Overall changes over time (simple trend).
All computations client-side using fetched data.

D) Data Quality & Bias page
Make it academically satisfying:
- “Reliability signals”:
   - internal consistency proxy: inter-dimension coherence (e.g., correlation structure)
   - test–retest proxy: repeated whisky ratings variability (if same whisky appears multiple times)
- “Rater bias & scale usage”:
   - do you use full scale or central tendency? (histogram of Overall)
   - “leniency/severity” over time
- “Context effects”:
   - compare Naked vs Full mean shifts
- “Missingness & coverage”:
   - which dimensions are often left at default?
- “Outlier review”:
   - highest/lowest residual drams with notes

E) Methodology & Measurement Theory page (the Martin-Kersting bait)
Write an elegant, structured mini-handbook with:
1) Construct definition: “What is ‘Overall’ here?”
2) Scale level & interpretation: ordinal vs interval; why sliders are practical
3) Measurement error & uncertainty:
   - why single ratings are noisy
   - how aggregation stabilizes
4) Reliability:
   - test–retest (same whisky different day)
   - inter-rater (panel variance, if available)
5) Validity:
   - content validity (dimensions cover the construct)
   - criterion validity (links to preference/repurchase)
   - construct validity (expected patterns: Nose→Taste→Overall)
6) Norming & benchmarking:
   - reference groups, selection bias, comparability
7) Ethics & data:
   - anonymization, consent, transparency
Include “Kersting Notes” callouts:
- short, witty but serious boxes (“Wenn man’s ganz streng sieht…”)

F) Export page (Research-friendly)
Keep existing export features; add:
- “Export for Research (CSV/JSON)” explanation
- “Variable dictionary” (what each column means)
- “Recommended analysis pipeline” (R/Python pseudo-steps as text, not actual code required)
- “Reproducibility checklist” (date, filters, inclusion criteria)

G) Kersting Corner / Glossary

- Likert vs slider

- Messinvarianz (als “Coming later”)
- Regression to the mean
- Halo effect
- Common method bias
- Panel composition
- Range restriction
- Simpson’s paradox (with a whisky-themed mini-example)
Keep it short and charming.
